## AI Ethics in Autonomous Weapons

This project explores the ethical challenges that come with using artificial intelligence in warfare, specifically autonomous weapons. The goal was to go beyond how these systems function and really think about what it means to let machines take part in decisions that can impact human life.

What stood out to me the most is how quickly technology is advancing in this space, while the ethical conversation still feels behind. Autonomous weapons are often discussed in terms of efficiency and reduced risk for soldiers, but there is a deeper question around responsibility that is not as clear.

What this project covers

We looked at core AI ethics principles such as accountability, fairness, and transparency, and applied them to real world use of autonomous weapons. One of the main areas we focused on was unintended consequences and who is affected when something goes wrong.

Unlike traditional systems, autonomous weapons can make decisions without direct human control. This raises concerns about accountability. If a system makes a mistake, it is not always clear who is responsible. The developer, the organization, or the system itself. That uncertainty becomes a serious issue when the stakes involve human lives.

We also looked at how these systems might behave in unpredictable environments. Even a highly accurate model cannot account for every real world scenario, and the cost of error in this context is not something that can be easily corrected.

## My perspective

While working on this, I kept questioning where we should draw the line.

There is no doubt that AI can support decision making and reduce certain risks, but fully removing human involvement from life and death decisions does not feel right. Even if the technology continues to improve, there is something important about human judgment, especially in situations that require context, empathy, and ethical reasoning.

At the same time, I do not think the solution is to stop using AI altogether. The challenge is finding a balance. AI can assist, but there should be clear boundaries where human oversight is required. Without that, we risk creating systems that are efficient but disconnected from the consequences of their actions.

## Why this matters

AI is moving into areas where decisions have real and irreversible outcomes. Autonomous weapons are one of the clearest examples of this. Thinking through these issues is important not just from a technical perspective, but from a human one.

For anyone working in AI or data, understanding these trade offs is part of the responsibility that comes with building these systems.

## Project format

This work was completed as part of a team presentation. The final deliverable includes slides and a recorded presentation where we discuss ethical principles, stakeholder impacts, and individual perspectives.
